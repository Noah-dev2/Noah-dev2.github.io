Brisket

Jewish immigrants brought their brisket traditions to America in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, where the availability of beef made it more accessible.

This recipe makes a delicious and tender Brisket. It’s easy, requires few steps, and cooks low and slow.

Brisket is a tough, flavorful cut of beef from a cow's lower chest that becomes incredibly tender when cooked properly.

Instructions
  • Preheat oven to 325°F.
  • Pat brisket dry and place in a snug roasting pan.
  • Mix Coca‑Cola, chili sauce, and onion soup mix; pour over brisket. Roast ~30 min per pound.
  • Rest brisket 10 minutes; skim fat from pan juices.
  • Trim fat, slice against the grain, and serve with warm pan juices.
Ingredients
  • 6 pounds of beef brisket
  • one 12‑oz can Coca‑Cola
  • 1 cup chili sauce
  • 1 envelope onion soup mix
